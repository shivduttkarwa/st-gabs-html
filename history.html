Our History

Before our school…

Dharug Country

Our school is built on Dharug Country. There were many Indigenous clans living in the area known as Dharug (Dharug being the dialect spoken in large parts of what is now the Western Sydney region), and those that called Castle Hill home (or “Mogoaillee” as the area was then known to indigenous Australians) were the Bidjigal people.

We respectfully acknowledge the ancestors and Elders past, present and emerging of the Bidjigal and Dharug people. Those who respected, cared for and called this land home for thousands of years before us.

Founded in 1922…

A hundred years of care

Over one hundred years ago (on 22nd September 1922) St Gabriel's School opened its doors to its first students. St Gabriel's started in an old homestead called Southleigh, as a boarding school for boys who were deaf. It soon moved into a larger brick structure built on that same land.

In 1973, it transitioned to a day school, and welcomed girls, continuing its work with students who were hearing impaired.

An integration program was introduced in 1976 where students attended St Gabriel's in the morning and their local mainstream school for the afternoon.

In 1979 a new orange brick school building was finished. It sat further toward the front of the block and closer to Old Northern Road. The land was subdivided and the old school buildings handed over to Parramatta Diocese to start a co-ed Secondary College (which is now known as Gilroy Catholic College and still neighbours us today). St Gabriel's continued to focus on primary education for students with deafness or hearing impairment.

In 2010, St Gabriel's criteria for admission was widened to include students with a range of special education needs, including children with vision impairment and Autism. At this time, the number of students was just 20.

The school started its transition to secondary in 2019, with its first Year 7 class. Major building works commenced in 2020 for the new Secondary School space.

In 2023 St Gabriel's will have completed its three new buildings and in 2024 the first Year 12 class will graduate. The school will have close to 200 students by the time it's K to 12.

While the school has gone through a number of changes over its hundred years, its mission today is still the same — nurturing independence and transforming the lives of its students.

Our History

A timeline of milestones

1920

Southleigh is purchased

The Christian Brothers find a suitable property at Castle Hill. Southleigh consisted of “a fine old residence, a cottage near the road and 35 acres of good land”.

1922

The school opens

Two Irish Christian Brothers arrive with plans for the new building. School begins on 22 September. The first student, Patrick Kenna, takes up residence.

1973

Day school begins

St Gabriel’s transitions to a day school and welcomes girls — a significant step toward a more inclusive community.

1976

Integration program

Students begin attending St Gabriel’s in the morning and their local mainstream school in the afternoon — pioneering inclusive education.

1979

New school building

A new orange brick building is completed. The land is subdivided and handed over to Parramatta Diocese — founding what is now Gilroy Catholic College, still our neighbour today.

2010

Broader admission

Admission widens to include students with vision impairment and Autism alongside hearing impairment. The school has just 20 students — but growth is coming.

2019

Secondary school begins

St Gabriel’s welcomes its first Year 7 class. Major building works commence in 2020, setting the stage for a full K–12 journey.

Today

K–12 community

Three new buildings, close to 200 students, and one unchanged mission — nurturing independence and transforming the lives of its students.

Strategic Plan

2026–2028

Our 2026–2028 Strategic Plan has been shaped through the ideas and contributions of our School community. Identifying four main priorities: Learning and Teaching, Faith and Identity, Community Wellbeing and Sustainability, the Plan reflects our commitment to providing a safe, inclusive and empowering environment for every student.

At St Gabriel’s School, our focus is on providing students with rich learning experiences and interactions that nurture independence and transform lives. Our students are at the heart of everything we do and it is our goal for them to feel valued, supported and inspired to thrive.

Drawing on the Edmund Rice Education Australia (EREA) Touchstones, our Strategic Plan sets a clear direction for the future and strengthens our place at the forefront of special education. It is grounded in the values that continue to shape who we are as a community.

Acknowledgement of Country

We want to respectfully acknowledge the Traditional Owners and Custodians of these lands, seas, and waters throughout Australia. We pay our respects to Elders both past and present. We acknowledge the history, the resilience and the continual contributions of Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander peoples of their Country.
